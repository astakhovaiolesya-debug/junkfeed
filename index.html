Common Manipulation Techniques:

Red Herring

Pulling attention away from the real issue by introducing something that feels relevant but actually has nothing to do with the original point.

Emotional Language

Words and phrases chosen to trigger strong negative feelings like fear, anger, or panic in the audience.

Cherry Picking

Selecting only the evidence that supports one side of an argument while quietly leaving out anything that tells a different story.

Misrepresentation

Taking real text, images, or video and placing them in a different situation or stripping away key background information to change what they actually mean.

Slippery Slope

Claiming that one small action will automatically trigger a chain of increasingly serious consequences, usually without any evidence to back it up.

Common Questions:

Disinformation is false or misleading information that is deliberately created and spread to deceive people. Unlike accidental misinformation, disinformation is intentional. It is designed to trigger emotional reactions like fear or outrage, making it more likely to be believed and shared before anyone stops to question it.

Ask yourself a few simple questions before engaging with any content. Does it make you feel fear, anger, or panic? Does it claim something dramatic that no major news outlet is reporting? Does it use vague sources or no sources at all?

Short-form video platforms like TikTok and Instagram use algorithms that prioritise content based on engagement. Every time you watch, rewatch, like, or comment on a video, even a misleading one, the algorithm reads it as a signal of interest and shows it to more people.

You have more power over your feed than you think. Use the Not Interested feature to tell the algorithm what you do not want to see. Report content that feels misleading or harmful.

Check the source before sharing anything that triggers a strong emotional reaction. Small actions add up. Every time you refuse to engage with junk, you starve the algorithm of the signal it needs to spread it further.
